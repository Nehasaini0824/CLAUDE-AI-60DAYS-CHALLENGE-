🧩 Just hit 475 points and earned the "Prompt Expert" badge on Prompt Puzzle — a game that teaches AI prompting through hands-on challenges instead of theory.
The real learning wasn't in getting things right — it was in the 3 wrong placements that taught me why certain prompts fail:
🔴 Causal claims need identification strategy — "ads caused more sales" isn't an answer until you've ruled out confounders and stated your assumptions.
🟡 Experiments need explicit statistical guardrails — power analysis, stopping rules, and side-metrics aren't optional extras, they're what separates a real A/B test from a coin flip.
🟢 Technical prompts reward specificity — assigning a role, demanding method comparisons, and requiring validation criteria turned "find anomalies in my data" into a rigorous, production-ready analysis plan (100% accuracy on that one 👇).
The pattern across every advanced scenario: vague prompts get vague answers. The moment you demand methodology, validation, and stated limitations, the AI's output quality jumps dramatically.
69% average accuracy, 18 moves, 267 seconds — but more importantly, a much sharper instinct for what separates a mediocre prompt from an expert one.
