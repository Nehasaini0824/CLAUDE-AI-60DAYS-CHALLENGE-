🚀 Day 32 of #60DaysClaudeAIChallenge

I built an AI tool that teaches marketing strategy instead of just generating content.
Most AI marketing tools spit out a content calendar and call it a day. This one is different — it walks you through the actual thinking strategists use, one decision at a time.
I ran it through a scenario: Maya Chen, a strength-training coach for busy parents, trying to build a personal brand from scratch.
Here's what the simulation covered:
🎯 Audience first — before touching a single platform, define who you're actually talking to and why they'd trust you.
📱 Platform strategy — not "be everywhere," but a scored breakdown of where your specific audience actually lives (LinkedIn 9/10, X 8/10 vs. Pinterest 2/10).
🧱 Content pillars — 3 recurring themes tied to real goals: Trust, Conversion, Engagement. Not random posting.
🗺️ A 30-day roadmap — organized by weekly strategic goals, not a list of individual posts.
⚡ A curveball event — because real marketing isn't just the plan, it's how you react when a competitor starts copying your format.
📊 A final growth report — scoring the strategy, flagging the best decision and the one with room to improve, plus the 3 lessons that mattered most:
1️⃣ Authenticity outperforms polish.
2️⃣ Consistency compounds.
3️⃣ Niche clarity turns strangers into an audience.
The best part? Every step comes with a reusable prompt, so you can run this same thinking process on your own brand.
Marketing isn't about having a tool that writes for you — it's about learning to think like the person who knows why the content works.
