🚚 Just ran my own supply chain through a live simulation — and the results were a great reality check.
I stepped into the shoes of a Supply Chain Ops lead at Haven Works, a bicycle company manufacturing carbon road frames for customers across Germany, France, Spain, and Italy.
Across 5 key decisions, here's the strategy I built:
🔹 Suppliers → Multiple Suppliers (resilience over cost)
🔹 Factory → Domestic Manufacturing (speed & quality control)
🔹 Warehousing → Regional Network (balanced coverage)
🔹 Transportation → Road/Trucking (flexible, reliable)
🔹 Inventory → Balanced Strategy (steady, no extremes)
📊 The result: 61/100 overall
✅ Strengths: Risk Management (85/100) and Delivery Speed (75/100)
⚠️ Weak spot: Cost Efficiency (20/100) — my biggest risk, since rising costs were squeezing margins
The recommended fixes were spot on:
1️⃣ Negotiate volume discounts / consolidate shipments
2️⃣ Shift some transport from air to rail/sea
3️⃣ Add a moderate safety stock buffer

Biggest takeaway: There's no "perfect" supply chain — every choice is a trade-off between cost, speed, risk, and sustainability. Optimizing one metric almost always pulls another down. Real operations leadership is about knowing which trade-offs your business can actually afford.
